About Alappuzha

Discover the Venice of the East - Kerala's backwater paradise

The Backwater Capital of India

Alappuzha, formerly known as Alleppey, is the administrative headquarters of Alappuzha district in Kerala. Famous for its backwaters, beaches, and lagoons, it's known as the "Venice of the East". The city's canals, backwaters, beaches, and lagoons have made it a top tourist destination.

Alappuzha is a land of immense natural beauty. The district is flanked by the Arabian Sea on the west and has a vast network of lakes, lagoons and freshwater rivers crisscrossing it. The snake boat races held here during Onam are internationally famous.

Houseboats (kettuvallams) are the most popular tourist attraction in Alappuzha. These floating accommodations offer a unique way to experience the tranquility of the backwaters while enjoying authentic Kerala cuisine. From bird watching to fishing, and from Ayurvedic treatments to village tours, Alappuzha offers unforgettable experiences.
